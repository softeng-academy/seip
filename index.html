YOUR LECTURE
In this course, you will learn about all essential concepts of Software Engineering and its "king discipline" Software Architecture, which are relevant in practice — from the specific perspective of the software industry. The course consists of 10 lecture modules, each 3 hours long, compressed into just 5 consecutive weeks in 2025/Q4 of WS25/26. Additionally, consultation hours are offered once per week.

This course is based on the lecture concept Multimedia Didactics, which was partly developed in the context of this course and for which the lecturer received the Balzert-Preis 2023 of the Gesellschaft f√ºr Informatik (GI) "for his outstanding contribution to the teaching of Computer Science."

YOUR PARTICIPATION
Except for the final exam, the course exclusively happens online. Two different types of communication are used, each deliberately optimizing a very different aspect of the course.

On the one hand, high-resolution 1080p60 (1920x1080 px, 60 fps) video streams are used for the lectures to combine the lecturer, content, and a Head-Up-Display (HUD). The lectures are delivered as YouTube Live Events in broadcasting format with a latency of about 2-3 seconds, where the display quality and features are optimized.

For technical reasons, you cannot have a direct voice interaction with the lecturer, but with the help of the companion application HUDS Pad, which is displayed in addition to the video stream, during the lectures you can use text messages to raise your questions, participate in the quizzes, give feedback via emojis and share your personal challenge and mood situation.

On the other hand, interactive Microsoft Teams Meetings are used, where your lecturer is delivered as a low-latency, but also as a low-resolution, 360p30 (640x360 px, 30 fps) video stream and the content is delivered separately as a high-resolution, but also high latency, 1080pX (1920x1080 px, 1-15 fps) video stream. Here, the direct personal communication is optimized, which is especially important for the consultation hours.

In other words, we intentionally use broadcasting events instead of interactive meetings in order to optimize the overall display quality and multimedia features of the lectures!
